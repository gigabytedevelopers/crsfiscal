What is FRL 2011?

Basically, the Cross River State Fiscal Responsibility Law (FRL 2011) is a template to ensure that revenue raising policies, resource allocation decisions and debt management decisions are undertaken in a transparent and timely fashion.

The Law also outlines measures to adopt in order to curb and reduce wasteful spending of public funds by Ministries, Department and Agencies (MDAs). In addition, the FRL 2011 set out principles that can boost and make better Internally Generated Revenue (IGR) in Cross River State.

Cross River State economic future and fiscal responsibility are directly linked. If we fail to quickly address the growing imbalance between federal commitments and available revenues, we will squander the only opportunity to get our finances in order which will directly impact governance.

Public Financial Management

Fiscal responsibility in public financial management is essential to creating a better, stronger, more prosperous State. The choices we make today or fail to make will determine what kind of future our children and grandchildren inherit.

About the projects

Garment Factory

Cross River Garment factory, the single largest garment floor in Nigeria has been in full operation since 2017, but yet to be fully patronized by Federal Government MDAs. The factory has near 3000 workers on shift dominantly women.

Rice Seedling Factory

The Hybrid Rice Seedling Factory built by the Cross River State Government at the cost of N3bn is estimated to generate at least N14bn annually. The factory, which was unveiled 2018 by President Muhammadu Buhari, is sited in Calabar.

Rice Mill Ogoja

This is Africa's first vitaminized rice mill which has just been established in Cross River State in the city of Ogoja. It currently engages almost 20,000 workers. The rice mill has a storage capacity of over 6,500 metric tonnes making it the biggest in Nigeria
